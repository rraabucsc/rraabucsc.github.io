10. The Replicator Equation

Consider the distribution over all possible strategies \(h\).

\begin{equation} \rho(h). \end{equation}

Interpret “fitness” as exponential growth rate (no mutation).

\begin{equation} - \mathscr{L}_{\rho}(h) = \frac{\dot{N}_{h}}{N_{h}} = \frac{\rm d}{{\rm d} t} \log N_{h}. \end{equation}

After re-normalization,

\begin{equation} \dot{\rho}(h) = \rho(h) \Big[ \overline{\mathscr{L}_{\rho}} - \mathscr{L}_{\rho}(h) \Big]. \qquad \overline{\mathscr{L}_{\rho}} = \sum_{h} \rho(h) \mathscr{L}_{\rho}(h). \end{equation}

11. Evolutionary Game Theory

Hawk-Dove (Game of Chicken)
\begin{align} \mathscr{L}(k) &= 2 \rho(k) - \rho(v).\\ \mathscr{L}(v) &= \phantom{2}\rho(k). \end{align}
Prisoner's Dilemma
\begin{align} \mathscr{L}(d) &= \phantom{2}\rho(d) - \rho(c).\\ \mathscr{L}(c) &= 2\rho(d). \end{align}
Rock-Paper-Scissors
\begin{align} \mathscr{L}(r) &= \rho(p) - \rho(s).\\ \mathscr{L}(p) &= \rho(s) - \rho(r). \\ \mathscr{L}(s) &= \rho(r) - \rho(p). \end{align}
Stable equilibrium at
\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix}\rho(k) \\ \rho(v)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\ 1\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation}
Stable equilibrium at
\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix}\rho(d) \\ \rho(c)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation}
Cyclic dynamics centered at
\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix}\rho(r) \\ \rho(p) \\ \rho(s) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1/3 \\ 1/3 \\ 1/3 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation}

Used in economics, biology [Friedman and Sinervo (2016)].

12. Bayesian Inference as Natural Selection

Interpret \(\rho(h)\) as distribution over hypotheses \(h\), loss as surprisal.

\begin{equation} \mathscr{L}(h) = -\log \Pr(x \mid h). \end{equation}

Discretized Replicator Equation:

\begin{equation} \rho^{t+1}(h) = \rho^{t}(h) \frac{\exp[-\mathscr{L}_{\rho^{t}}(h)]}{\overline {\exp[-\mathscr{L}_{\rho^{t}}]}}. \end{equation}

Bayes’ Theorem:

\begin{equation} P(h \mid x) = P(h) \frac{\displaystyle P(x \mid h) }{\displaystyle P(x)}. \end{equation}

\(\Pr(x \mid h)\) consistent with “probability of survival” for \(h\) in state \(x\).

Generalized by Theorems 3, 4.

14. Gradient Descent

ML’s work-horse algorithm:

\begin{equation} \dot{\theta}^{i} = - \eta \partial_{i} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}). \end{equation}

Problem: Left, right sides of equation live in different vector spaces!

\begin{equation} [\dot{\theta}^{i}] = \frac{\mathsf{U}}{\mathsf{T}}. \qquad [\partial_{i} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta})] = \frac{\mathsf{L}}{\mathsf{U}}. \qquad [\eta] = \frac{\color{red}{\mathsf{U}^{2}}}{\mathsf{LT}}. \end{equation}

Dynamics vary with change of basis for \(\boldsymbol{\theta}\).

15. Iterative Refinement

\begin{equation} \dot{\boldsymbol{\theta}} = \underset{\mathbf{u}}{\rm argmin} \bigg[ \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + \sum_{i} u^{i} \partial_{i} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + {\rm D}(\mathbf{u}) \bigg]. \end{equation}

When \({\rm D}\) is Euclidean,

\begin{equation} {\rm D}(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{1}{2 \eta} \sum_{i,j} u^{i} \delta_{ij} u^{j}. \end{equation}

Solve for minimum:

\begin{equation} \partial_{i} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + \frac{1}{\eta} \sum_{j} \delta_{ij} u^{j} = 0. \end{equation}

Vanilla gradient descent.

\begin{equation} \dot{\theta}^{i} = - \eta \sum_{j} \delta^{ij} \partial_{j} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}). \end{equation}

What other choices can we make for \(\rm D\)?

16. Alternative Metrics \({\boldsymbol{\rm D}}\)

Metric can depend on:

  1. The parameters \(\boldsymbol{\theta}\).

    • Euclidean, Mahalanobis.

  2. Previous updates.

    • Momentum, Nesterov acceleration.

  3. \(\bbox[#ddd]{\text{The function } f_{\boldsymbol{\theta}} \colon \mathcal{X} \to \mathcal{Y}}.\)
    • Natural or covariant gradient.
    • Independent of parameterization.
    • \(\bbox[#ddd]{\text{Probability distributions } \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}} \text{ over } \mathcal{H}}.\)

Many flavors to choose from

17. The Fisher-Rao Metric

Parameterize probability distributions \(\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}\) over \(\mathcal{H}\).

Measure size of \(\dot{\boldsymbol{\theta}}\) by marginal information about \(\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}\).


\begin{equation} {\rm D}_{KL}\big( \rho_{(\boldsymbol{\theta} + \mathbf{u})} \parallel \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}} \big) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} u^{i} F_{ij}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) u^{j} + \mathcal{O}(|\mathbf{u}|^{3}). \end{equation}
\begin{equation} F_{ij}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) = \frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial u^{i} \partial u^{j}} {\rm D}_{KL}\big( \rho_{(\boldsymbol{\theta} + \mathbf{u})} \parallel \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}} \big) = \underset{H \sim \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}{\rm Cov} \Big[ \partial_{i} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(H), \partial_{j} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(H)\Big]. \end{equation}

18. Natural Gradient Descent

\begin{equation} \dot{\boldsymbol{\theta}} = \underset{\mathbf{u}}{\rm argmin} \bigg[ \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + \sum_{i} u^{i} \partial_{i} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + {\rm D}(\mathbf{u}) \bigg]. \end{equation}

Euclidean Metric \(\qquad \implies\)

Vanilla gradient descent.

\begin{equation} {\rm D}(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} u^{i} \delta_{ij} u^{j}. \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \dot{\theta}^{i} = - \sum_{j} \delta^{ij} \partial_{j} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}). \end{equation}

Fisher Divergence \(\qquad \implies\)

Fisher-Rao natural gradient descent (FR-NGD).

\begin{equation} {\rm D}(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} u^{i} F_{ij}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) u^{j}. \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \dot{\theta}^{i} = - \sum_{j} \big[ F(\boldsymbol{\theta})^{\dagger} \big]^{ij} \partial_{j} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}). \end{equation}

20. Main Result

Theorem 1:
FR-NGD of population loss minimizes Fisher divergence from replicator dynamics.
\begin{equation} \dot{\boldsymbol{\theta}} = {\rm argmin}\quad \frac{1}{2} \sum_{h, k} \Big(\dot{\rho}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h) - \dot{\rho}^{\star}(h)\Big) F[\rho](h, k) \Big(\dot{\rho}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(k) - \dot{\rho}^{\star}(k)\Big). \end{equation}

Replicator Dynamics:

\begin{equation} \dot{\rho}^{\star}(h) = \rho(h) \Big[ \overline{\mathscr{L}_{\rho}} - \mathscr{L}_{\rho}(h) \Big]. \end{equation}

Fisher-Rao Natural Gradient Descent of \(\mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) = \overline{\mathscr{L}_{\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}}\):

\begin{align} \dot{\theta}^{i} &= - \sum_{j} \big[ F(\boldsymbol{\theta})^{\dagger} \big]^{ij} \partial_{j} \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}). \\[0.5em] \dot{\rho}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h) &= \sum_{i} \frac{\partial \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h)}{\partial \theta^{i}} \dot{\theta}^{i}. \end{align}

22. Least-Squares Optimality

\begin{equation} \sum_{j} F_{ij}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) \dot{\theta}^{j} = - \partial_{i} \overline{\mathscr{L}_{\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}}. \end{equation}

Normal equation of a least-squares problem, \(\sum_{h,j} A^{ih} A_{hj} x^{j} = \sum_{h} A^{ih} b_{h}\):

\begin{equation} \sum_{h,j} \Big[ \partial_{i} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h)\Big] \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h) \Big[ \partial_{j} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h)\Big] \dot{\theta}^{j} = -\sum_{h} \Big[ \partial_{i} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h)\Big] \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h) \mathscr{L}_{\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}(h). \end{equation}

A projection of the replicator equation:

\begin{equation} \sum_{h} \Big[\partial_{i} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h)\Big] \dot{\rho}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h) = \sum_{h} \Big[ \partial_{i} \log \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h)\Big] \rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}(h) \Big(\overline{\mathscr{L}_{\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}} - \mathscr{L}_{\rho_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}(h) \Big). \end{equation}

Theorem 2: \(~\) linear combinations of score match replicator dynamics.

24. Real-World Applications

An alternative to simulated evolution

Pros:

  • No need to simulate genes!
  • Continuous, high-dimensional \(\mathcal{H}\).
    • Avoid high-dimensional PDE.
  • Highly non-convex \(\mathscr{L}_{\rho}(h)\).
  • Robust to reparameterization.
    • Choice of \(\rho_{\theta}\) obviates mutation.
    • Analytic \(F^{\dagger}\) in some cases.

Cons:

  • \(F^{\dagger}\) is \(\mathcal{O}^{\approx 2.37}(n)\), in general.
    • Faster approximations possible.
    • An active area of research.

29. Stochastic Annealing

How would switching policy increase loss for \(u\) observing \(v\)?

\begin{equation} S = {\rm E}\Big[\mathscr{L}_{v}(\text{opposite policy}) - \mathscr{L}_{v}(\text{same policy})\Big]. \quad (\text{want to maximize}) \end{equation}

Learning Rule:

\begin{align} \text{Switch to opposite policy} \quad &\pi(u, v) \leftarrow 1 - \pi(u, v), \\ \text{with probability} \quad &\frac{1}{2}\max\big[1, e^{-\beta S}\big]. \end{align}

\(\beta > 0.\)

38. Symmetry

Calculus of variations with non-local Lagrangian!

\begin{equation} \forall q, \quad (\delta \mathcal{L})[q] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} g(u,v) \frac{\partial \ell \big( a(u, v), a(v, u) ) \big) }{\partial f(u)} q(u) {\rm d}{v} {\rm d}{u}. \end{equation}

Symmetric \(\ell\), \(\varepsilon \ll 0\), ensures

\begin{equation} \forall u, \quad g\Big(u, f(u)\Big) \Big(2a\big(f(u), u\big) - 1\Big) = 0. \end{equation}

This implies symmetry of \(f\):

\begin{equation} f(f(u)) = u. \end{equation}
